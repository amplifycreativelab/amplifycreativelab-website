When customers can't touch a product before buying, the next best thing is being able to spin, rotate, and examine it from every angle. 360° product photography and video give online shoppers the confidence of an in-store experience—reducing purchase anxiety and cart abandonment.

For Perth e-commerce brands competing nationally and globally, interactive product imagery isn't just premium—it's increasingly expected. This guide covers everything from basic concepts to implementation strategies.

## Understanding 360° Product Photography

### What Is 360° Product Photography?

360° product photography captures your product from all angles—typically every 5° to 15° around a full rotation. These individual frames (usually 24-72 images) combine into an interactive viewer that lets customers spin the product with their mouse or finger.

**The experience simulates:**
- Picking up a product in-store
- Turning it over to examine all sides
- Checking build quality and details
- Understanding true shape and proportion

Unlike static photography that shows one carefully chosen angle, 360° reveals everything—which builds trust.

### 360° Photography vs Product Video

Both enhance product pages, but serve different purposes:

| Feature | 360° Photography | Product Video |
|---------|------------------|---------------|
| **User control** | Customer controls rotation | Linear playback |
| **Detail focus** | Examine specific angles | Guided tour of features |
| **Production** | Turntable, consistent frames | Movement, possibly hand-held |
| **File size** | Multiple images (larger total) | Single video file |
| **Best for** | Product examination | Demonstrations, storytelling |
| **Interaction** | High engagement, active | Passive viewing |

**The ideal approach:** Use 360° photography for detail examination and product video for context, demonstrations, and emotional connection.

### Why It Works

360° product imagery addresses key online shopping barriers:

**1. The "What's it really like?" question**
Static photos can hide imperfections or mislead about proportions. 360° reveals everything, setting accurate expectations.

**2. Touch and feel simulation**
While we can't replicate texture online, letting customers rotate products mimics the physical examination process.

**3. Detail verification**
Customers can check specific features—logos, closures, finishes—from the angle that matters to them.

**4. Reduced uncertainty**
The more information customers have, the more confident their purchase decision. Confidence correlates with conversion.

## Equipment for 360° Product Photography

### The Turntable Setup

The core of 360° photography is consistent rotation:

**Manual turntables ($30-100):**
- User rotates product manually
- Mark increments (every 10° or 15°)
- Slower but affordable
- Best for small product volumes

**Motorised turntables ($150-500):**
- Electric rotation with stops
- Consistent timing between frames
- Often includes software integration
- Faster workflow for batches

**Professional systems ($1,000-10,000+):**
- Integrated camera triggering
- Precise step intervals
- Software automation
- High-volume production

### Camera Requirements

360° photography requires consistent exposure across all frames:

**Essentials:**
- Manual mode control (no auto-exposure shifts)
- Remote triggering capability
- Tethered shooting preferred
- Adequate resolution (24MP+)

**Ideal:**
- Mirrorless or DSLR camera
- 50mm or 85mm lens for product work
- Locked focus (manual after initial focus)
- Fixed white balance

**Acceptable:**
- Higher-end smartphone (with app control)
- Compact camera with manual modes
- Consistent artificial lighting essential

### Lighting for 360°

Lighting consistency is critical—shadows shouldn't shift as the product rotates:

**Recommended setup:**
- Overhead softbox for main light
- Fill lights or reflectors on both sides
- Background light if using seamless
- Diffusion panels for even coverage

**Avoid:**
- Directional side lighting (creates rotating shadows)
- Single-point sources (harsh shadows)
- Mixed lighting (colour inconsistencies)
- Natural light (changes throughout session)

### Backgrounds

**White seamless** (most common):
- Professional, e-commerce standard
- Easy post-processing
- Works for all product types

**Coloured backgrounds:**
- Brand-specific colours
- Requires consistent lighting
- Can add visual interest

**Transparent/gradient:**
- Created in post-processing
- Provides flexibility
- Works with different website backgrounds

## The 360° Photography Workflow

### Pre-Production

**1. Product preparation:**
- Clean all products thoroughly
- Remove tags, stickers, price labels
- Steam fabrics
- Polish reflective surfaces
- Check for defects

**2. Setup testing:**
- Position turntable and camera
- Set lighting for even coverage
- Test full rotation for shadows
- Capture test sequence
- Review and adjust

**3. Camera settings:**
- Shoot in RAW for flexibility
- Lock exposure settings (manual mode)
- Set fixed white balance (not auto)
- Lock focus after initial setup
- Use remote trigger or timed capture

### Capture Process

**Standard workflow:**

1. **Position product** on turntable centre
2. **Initial focus** and lock
3. **Test shot** and review
4. **Capture sequence:**
   - 36 frames = 10° increments
   - 72 frames = 5° increments
   - Consistent timing between frames
5. **Review sequence** for any issues
6. **Repeat** for next product

**Time estimates:**
- Simple product: 10-15 minutes
- Complex styling: 20-30 minutes
- Batch shooting: 5-10 minutes per product once setup stable

### Post-Production

**Per-frame editing:**
- White balance consistency
- Exposure matching
- Background cleanup
- Dust and imperfection removal
- Colour matching across frames

**Batch processing:**
- Apply consistent adjustments to all frames
- Export at required dimensions
- Optimise file sizes (JPEG/WebP, 60-80% quality)
- Consistent naming convention

**Assembly:**
- Import to 360° viewer software
- Set rotation speed and behaviour
- Test interaction
- Export final viewer or files

### File Management

**Naming convention:**
`ProductName_Frame01.jpg` through `ProductName_Frame36.jpg`

**Organisation:**
```
/360 Photography
  /Product Name
    /Raw Files
    /Edited Frames
    /Final Viewer
```

**File size targets:**
- Individual frames: 50-150KB each
- Total sequence: Under 2MB for fast loading
- WebP format preferred for web use

## Creating Product Videos

### Types of Product Videos

**1. Rotation video (automated):**
- Product rotates on turntable
- Clean, professional look
- Can be created from 360° frames
- 10-30 seconds typical

**2. Demonstration video:**
- Shows product in use
- Highlights features and benefits
- More complex production
- 30-90 seconds typical

**3. Lifestyle video:**
- Product in context/setting
- Emotional connection focus
- Higher production value
- 15-60 seconds typical

**4. Unboxing/reveal:**
- Packaging to product journey
- Premium brand experience
- Instagram/TikTok friendly
- 15-45 seconds typical

### Video Equipment Basics

**Camera options:**
- Modern smartphones (4K capable)
- Mirrorless cameras for best quality
- Webcams for quick social content
- Action cameras for unique angles

**Audio (if needed):**
- External microphone preferred
- Reduce background noise
- Consider adding music in post

**Accessories:**

### Product Video Best Practices

**Technical standards:**
- 4K resolution when possible (future-proof)

## Implementing 360° on Your Website

### 360° Viewer Options

**Hosted solutions (easiest):**
- **Sirv** - Popular, good free tier
- **Cloudimage** - CDN integration
- **Magic360** - WordPress integration
- **Spin Studio** - Simple setup

**Self-hosted solutions:**
- **ThreeSixty.js** - Open source
- **jQuery Reel** - Lightweight
- **SpriteSpin** - Feature-rich

**E-commerce platform integrations:**
- Shopify: Apps available (Sirv, others)
- WooCommerce: Plugins available
- BigCommerce: Built-in support
- Custom: JavaScript implementations

### Performance Considerations

Large image sequences can slow your site:

**Optimisation strategies:**
- Compress individual frames (60-80% JPEG quality)
- Use WebP format where supported
- Lazy load 360° viewers (load on scroll/interaction)
- Preload only first few frames initially
- Cache properly with CDN

**Target performance:**
- Total sequence under 2MB
- Initial load under 500KB
- Remaining frames on interaction
- Maintain [Core Web Vitals](/blog/core-web-vitals-hospitality-websites-explained-simply/) standards

### Mobile Experience

360° must work well on mobile devices:

**Mobile optimisation:**
- Touch-friendly rotation controls
- Lower frame count for mobile (24 vs 72)
- Smaller image dimensions
- Prominent interaction prompt ("Spin to explore")
- Consider pinch-to-zoom capability

## Advanced 360° Techniques

### Multi-Row 360° (Hemispherical)

For products where top and bottom views matter:

**Standard 360°:** Single horizontal rotation
**Multi-row:** Multiple rotations at different angles

**When to use:**
- Shoes (top and sole views)
- Bags (standing and laid down)
- Complex electronics
- Premium products where detail justifies cost

**Increased complexity:**
- Multiple turntable positions
- More frames (72-216+ total)
- Higher production cost
- Larger file sizes

### Zoom Functionality

Allow customers to zoom into details:

- Capture at higher resolution
- Implement zoom viewer overlay
- Focus on key detail areas
- Particularly valuable for jewellery, watches

### Annotated 360° Views

Add interactive hotspots:

- Clickable points on product
- Pop-up feature descriptions
- Direct links to specifications
- Enhanced education and conversion
