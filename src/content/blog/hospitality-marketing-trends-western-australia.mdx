<h2>1. Hyper‑Local SEO & Google Business Profile Dominance</h2>

<p>
  <strong>Hyper‑local SEO</strong> is the practice of optimizing your online presence for extremely specific geographic areas—think suburbs like Northbridge, Leederville, Fremantle, Scarborough, Cottesloe, or even individual streets and landmarks. With Google's increasing emphasis on "near me" searches, venues that master hyper‑local SEO capture more high‑intent customers at the moment they're ready to dine.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>Data point:</strong> A 2024 BrightLocal study found that <strong>46% of all Google searches have local intent</strong>, and 76% of people who conduct a local search visit a business within 24 hours. For Perth venues, this means appearing in the "local pack" (the map with three business listings) can directly translate to foot traffic and bookings.
</p>

<aside className="faq-section">
  <h3>How much should I budget for hospitality marketing in 2025?</h3>
  <p>
    A good rule of thumb is to allocate <strong>5–10% of your projected revenue to marketing</strong>. For a venue doing $500k annually, that's $25,000–$50,000/year. Focus on high‑impact areas like professional photography, website performance, and local SEO first, as these provide long‑term compounding returns.
  </p>

  <h3>Which trend is most important for a new venue opening in Perth?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Hyper‑local SEO and Google Business Profile optimisation</strong> should be your top priority. They deliver immediate visibility to customers searching for venues in your area, which is critical during your launch phase. Build your GBP before you open, upload 50+ professional photos in the first month, and post weekly updates.
  </p>

  <h3>Can I implement these trends without a large marketing team?</h3>
  <p>
    Absolutely. Many tools and platforms are designed for small businesses with limited resources. Start with one trend—such as optimizing your GBP or posting weekly Reels—and gradually incorporate others. Partnering with an agency that understands both photography and development (like us) can also streamline the process and reduce your workload significantly.
  </p>

  <h3>How do I find the right influencers for my Perth venue?</h3>
  <p>
    Search Instagram and TikTok for hashtags like #PerthFood, #PerthEats, #FremantleFood, or #NorthbridgeRestaurants. Look for influencers who already post about your cuisine type, have engaged local followers (check comment quality), and whose aesthetic matches your brand. Start with micro-influencers (1,000–10,000 followers) for best ROI.
  </p>

  <h3>Is TikTok worth it for restaurants targeting older demographics?</h3>
  <p>
    TikTok's audience is ageing up—30% of users are now over 35. However, if your primary demographic is 50+, focus on Instagram Reels and Facebook. The video-first content strategy applies across platforms; just adjust where you post based on where your customers are.
  </p>

  <h3>How do I communicate sustainability without greenwashing?</h3>
  <p>
    Be specific and honest. Instead of vague claims like "eco-friendly," say "We source all seafood from Fremantle Fishing Boat Harbour, supporting local fishermen." Document your supplier relationships with photography. Display certifications where applicable. Authenticity is key—customers can spot empty claims quickly.
  </p>
