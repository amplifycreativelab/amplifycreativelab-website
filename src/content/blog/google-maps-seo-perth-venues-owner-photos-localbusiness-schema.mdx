<h2>The Power of Owner Photos</h2>

<p>Owner photos are images you upload directly to your Google Business Profile (GBP). Unlike customer‑uploaded photos, these are curated, professional, and tell a cohesive brand story. Google treats owner photos as a strong trust signal because they demonstrate an active, invested business owner.</p>

<h3>What Counts as an Owner Photo?</h3>

<p>Any photo you add via the "Photos" tab in your GBP dashboard is considered an owner photo. This includes:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Exterior and interior shots</strong> that showcase your venue's ambiance—crucial for Perth's diverse venue styles from industrial Leederville conversions to heritage Fremantle buildings.</li>
  <li><strong>Professional food and drink photography</strong> that makes dishes look irresistible and sets accurate expectations.</li>
  <li><strong>Behind‑the‑scenes images</strong> of your team, kitchen, or event setup that humanise your brand.</li>
  <li><strong>Menu boards, specials boards, and signage</strong> that highlight current offers and seasonal Perth produce.</li>
  <li><strong>Atmosphere shots</strong>—sunset views from Cottesloe venues, city lights from CBD rooftop bars, or busy Saturday night scenes.</li>
</ul>

<p>Google's algorithm evaluates the <strong>quantity, quality, and recency</strong> of these photos. A steady stream of high‑resolution, well‑lit images tells Google that your business is active, relevant, and worthy of being shown to more users.</p>

<h2>LocalBusiness Schema: The Invisible SEO Booster</h2>

<p>While owner photos work on your Google Business Profile, LocalBusiness schema works on your website. It's a piece of code (JSON‑LD) that you add to your site's HTML to explicitly tell Google your business name, address, phone number, opening hours, cuisine, price range, and other critical details.</p>

<h3>What Is LocalBusiness Schema?</h3>

<p><strong>LocalBusiness schema</strong> is a type of structured data that defines your venue as a local entity. It answers Google's questions before they're even asked: "Where is this business?" "What does it serve?" "When is it open?" "How expensive is it?" By providing this information in a machine‑readable format, you make it easier for Google to index and display your venue in relevant local searches.</p>

<p>For restaurants, cafés, and bars, you can use more specific schema types such as <code>Restaurant</code>, <code>CafeOrCoffeeShop</code>, or <code>BarOrPub</code>, which inherit all LocalBusiness properties and add cuisine, acceptsReservations, and servesCuisine fields.</p>

<h3>Schema Example for a Perth Restaurant</h3>

<p>Here's a simplified example of what Restaurant schema looks like for a Northbridge venue:</p>

<pre><code>{`{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Restaurant",
  "name": "Example Northbridge Bistro",
  "image": "https://yourdomain.com/images/restaurant-hero.jpg",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 William Street",
    "addressLocality": "Northbridge",
    "addressRegion": "WA",
    "postalCode": "6003",
    "addressCountry": "AU"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": -31.9478,
    "longitude": 115.8613
  },
  "telephone": "+61-8-9xxx-xxxx",
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "servesCuisine": ["Modern Australian", "Italian"],
  "acceptsReservations": true,
  "openingHoursSpecification": [...],
  "menu": "https://yourdomain.com/menu"
}`}</code></pre>

<p>This code tells Google everything it needs to know about your venue, increasing the likelihood of appearing for searches like "Italian restaurant Northbridge" or "dinner reservations Perth CBD."</p>

<h3>How Schema Affects Google Maps Visibility</h3>

<p>Google Maps and Google Search are tightly integrated. When your website includes LocalBusiness schema, Google can:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Verify your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency</strong> across the web, a major local‑ranking factor.</li>
  <li><strong>Display rich snippets</strong> in search results, such as star ratings, price indicators, and "Open now" badges.</li>
  <li><strong>Understand your menu</strong> if you also implement <code>Menu</code> and <code>MenuItem</code> schema, potentially triggering dish‑specific rich results.</li>
  <li><strong>Increase your chances of appearing in the local pack</strong>—the map‑based results that appear above organic listings for "best [cuisine] near me" searches.</li>
  <li><strong>Associate your website</strong> with your Google Business Profile, reinforcing your authority.</li>
</ul>
