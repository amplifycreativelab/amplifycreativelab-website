<h2>Frequently Asked Questions: Hospitality Digital Toolkit</h2>

<aside className="faq-section">
  <h3>What's the most important digital tool for a Perth restaurant?</h3>
  <p>
    Your <strong>POS system</strong> is the most critical—it's the operational backbone. But for driving new customers, your <strong>website and photography</strong> are equally essential. A beautiful, fast website with professional photos converts browsers into bookings; a great POS runs the service smoothly once they arrive.
  </p>

  <h3>How much should I budget for a complete digital toolkit?</h3>
  <p>
    A basic toolkit (POS, reservation, email marketing) runs <strong>$150–$300/month</strong> in subscriptions. Add a professional website ($3,000–$8,000 one-time) and annual photography refreshes ($1,500–$3,000), and you're looking at <strong>$6,000–$12,000 in year one</strong>, then ongoing subscriptions after that. The ROI typically covers this within 2–3 months through increased efficiency and bookings.
  </p>

  <h3>Should I use the same system for POS and reservations?</h3>
  <p>
    Some venues prefer all-in-one solutions for simplicity, but <strong>best-of-breed combinations</strong> often outperform. A strong POS (like Lightspeed) paired with a strong reservation system (like Resy) gives you more flexibility and typically better features in each category—as long as they integrate well.
  </p>

  <h3>How do I know if my tools are properly integrated?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Test the data flow:</strong> A booking made online should appear in your table management. A menu change in your POS should reflect on your website. Customer data should sync between your reservation system and email marketing. If you're manually copying information anywhere, there's an integration gap.
  </p>

  <h3>Do I need different tools for a café vs a fine-dining restaurant?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Yes.</strong> A quick-service café in Leederville may thrive with Square's simplicity and no formal reservation system. A fine-dining restaurant in Perth CBD needs Lightspeed's advanced menu engineering and Resy's high-end booking experience. Choose tools appropriate to your service style and customer expectations.
  </p>

  <h3>How often should I review and update my tech stack?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Annually at minimum.</strong> Technology evolves quickly, and new integrations emerge. Review at the start of each year: Are your tools still serving you? Are there better options? Is everything still connected properly? Major menu changes or venue expansions are also good trigger points.
