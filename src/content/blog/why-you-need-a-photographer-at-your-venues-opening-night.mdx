<ol>
  <li><strong>Create a shot list:</strong> Identify must‑have moments (ribbon‑cutting, first toast, signature dish presentation) and key details (decor, lighting, branded elements).</li>
  <li><strong>Schedule a walk‑through:</strong> Show your photographer the space beforehand so they can scout angles and lighting conditions.</li>
  <li><strong>Coordinate with staff:</strong> Ensure your team knows the photographer is there and encourage them to facilitate candid moments rather than posing stiffly.</li>
  <li><strong>Think beyond the party:</strong> Ask your photographer to capture empty‑venue shots before guests arrive—these are perfect for website hero images and future promotional materials.</li>
  <li><strong>Discuss usage rights:</strong> Make sure you have the rights to use the images across all your marketing channels, both now and in the future.</li>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions: Opening Night Photography</h2>

<aside className="faq-section">
  <h3>How much does opening night photography cost in Perth?</h3>
  <p>
    Professional event photography for a Perth venue launch typically ranges from <strong>$600–$1,500</strong> for 2–4 hours of coverage, including editing and web-optimised delivery. This investment generates 100–150+ images that fuel 3–6 months of marketing.
  </p>

  <h3>How many images should I expect from an opening night?</h3>
  <p>
    For a typical 3–4 hour launch event, expect <strong>100–150 edited images</strong>. This includes wide venue shots, candid guests, food/drink details, team moments, and pre-event empty venue shots.
  </p>

  <h3>When will I receive my images after the event?</h3>
  <p>
    Professional photographers should deliver within <strong>48–72 hours</strong> for a preview set (20–30 best images) and within <strong>5–7 days</strong> for the full edited gallery. Faster turnaround helps you capitalise on launch momentum.
  </p>

  <h3>Can I use the photos everywhere—website, social, print?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Yes—ensure your contract includes full usage rights.</strong> Reputable hospitality photographers provide unlimited commercial usage rights for your business. Avoid photographers who charge extra per use case or platform.
  </p>

  <h3>Should the photographer attend a walk-through before the event?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Highly recommended.</strong> A walk-through helps the photographer scout angles, understand lighting conditions, and identify the best spots for key shots. For Northbridge laneways or moody Fremantle venues, this is especially valuable.
  </p>

  <h3>What if my venue is very dark or has unusual lighting?</h3>
  <p>
    Professional event photographers bring <strong>equipment designed for low-light conditions</strong>—fast lenses, high-ISO cameras, and subtle lighting techniques. Look for portfolio examples in similar moody environments to ensure they can handle your space.
  </p>

  <h3>Is it worth also getting video at my opening night?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>If budget allows, yes.</strong> Short-form video (Reels, TikTok) is increasingly important for discovery. Some photographers offer combined packages; alternatively, a dedicated videographer can capture complementary content.
  </p>
</aside>
