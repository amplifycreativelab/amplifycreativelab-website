<p>
  Packshot photography refers to the systematic, studio‑based photography of products, usually against a plain background (most commonly white). The goal is to present the product clearly, consistently, and without distractions, making it ideal for e‑commerce listings, catalogues, and advertising.
</p>

<p>
  There are three primary types of packshots every WA brand should consider:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Clean white‑background shots:</strong> The standard for online retail. These images isolate the product, remove visual clutter, and meet platform requirements (like Amazon, eBay, or Shopify).
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Lifestyle contextual shots:</strong> Show the product in a realistic setting—a bottle of WA olive oil on a rustic kitchen table, a craft beer at a coastal pub, or a packaged sauce beside fresh ingredients. These shots help customers imagine using the product in their own lives.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Detail and scale shots:</strong> Close‑ups that highlight texture, ingredients, labelling, or unique features. For food and beverage products, this might include a drizzle of sauce, condensation on a bottle, or the crumb structure of baked goods.
  </li>
</ul>

<p>
  A complete packshot session will produce a suite of images that work together across your website, social media, retailer pages, and print materials.
</p>

<h2>The Packshot Photography Process: From Brief to Delivery</h2>

<p>
  A successful packshot shoot follows a structured workflow. Here’s what you can expect when working with a professional photographer:
</p>

<ol>
  <li>
    <strong>Consultation and brief:</strong> We discuss your products, brand story, target audience, and intended use for the images. This step ensures the final shots align with your marketing goals.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Styling and preparation:</strong> Products are cleaned, labels are straightened, and any props or surfaces are selected to match your brand aesthetic. For food products, this may involve working with a food stylist to achieve the perfect look.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Lighting and shooting:</strong> Using controlled studio lighting, we capture each product from multiple angles, ensuring consistent exposure, colour accuracy, and sharpness. We shoot tethered to a computer so you can review images in real time.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Editing and retouching:</strong> Images are colour‑corrected, backgrounds are cleaned up, and any minor imperfections are removed. The goal is a polished, professional result that still looks authentic.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Delivery and formatting:</strong> You receive a set of high‑resolution files ready for print, plus web‑optimised versions (WebP and JPEG) sized for e‑commerce platforms, social media, and your website.
  </li>
</ol>

<p>
  This end‑to‑end process ensures you get a complete visual library that can be used across all your marketing channels for months or even years.
</p>

<h3>Technical Considerations: Lighting, Composition, and Equipment</h3>

<p>
  Professional packshot photography relies on technical precision. Key elements include:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Lighting:</strong> Even, diffused lighting eliminates harsh shadows and hotspots, revealing true product colours and details. Multiple light sources are often used to create depth and dimension.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Composition:</strong> The product should be the undisputed hero of the frame. Rule‑of‑thirds, leading lines, and negative space are employed to create balanced, engaging images.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Equipment:</strong> High‑resolution cameras, macro lenses for detail shots, tripods for stability, and light‑tenting setups ensure consistent, sharp results. Tethered shooting allows for immediate quality control.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Colour accuracy:</strong> Using colour‑checker cards and calibrated monitors guarantees that the images you receive match your product’s true colours—critical for maintaining brand integrity.
  </li>
</ul>

<p>
  While smartphone cameras have improved, they cannot replicate the controlled environment and technical expertise of a professional studio. For packshots that meet commercial standards, professional equipment and know‑how are non‑negotiable.
</p>

<h2>Optimizing Packshots for E‑commerce and Digital Platforms</h2>

<p>
  Great packshots are only effective if they’re delivered in the right formats and sizes for their intended use. Here’s how to ensure your images perform online:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>File formats:</strong> Use WebP for the web (with JPEG fallbacks) to maintain quality while reducing file size. PNG is suitable for graphics with transparency, but for photographs, WebP is the modern standard.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Dimensions and resolution:</strong> E‑commerce platforms have specific requirements (often 2000px on the longest side). Your photographer should provide images resized for each platform, as well as smaller thumbnails for faster loading.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Alt text and filenames:</strong> Descriptive, keyword‑rich alt text (e.g., “organic WA honey jar on white background – Perth product photography”) improves accessibility and SEO. Filenames should be human‑readable, not generic codes.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Responsive images:</strong> Implement <code>srcset</code> attributes so your website serves appropriately sized images based on the user’s device, improving page speed—a key ranking factor.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Compression:</strong> Images should be compressed without visible quality loss. Aim for file sizes under 300KB for web use to keep your site fast and user‑friendly.
  </li>
</ul>

<h2>Common Mistakes WA Brands Make</h2>

<p>
  Avoid these frequent pitfalls that can undermine your product imagery:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Relying on smartphone photos:</strong> While convenient, phone cameras struggle with consistent lighting, colour accuracy, and detail capture. The result often looks amateurish and fails to compete with professional competitors.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Inconsistent backgrounds and lighting:</strong> Shooting products at different times or locations leads to a mismatched set that weakens your brand’s visual identity.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Over‑editing:</strong> Excessive retouching can make products look artificial or misleading, eroding consumer trust. The goal is enhancement, not alteration.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Ignoring platform specs:</strong> Uploading images that are too small, the wrong aspect ratio, or incorrectly formatted can cause them to display poorly on retailer sites, hurting conversions.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Skipping the lifestyle shots:</strong> Clean white‑background shots are essential, but lifestyle imagery is what connects emotionally with customers. Don’t miss this opportunity to tell your brand’s story.
  </li>
</ul>

<p>
  Investing in a professional packshot session from the start helps you avoid these mistakes and present your products in the best possible light.
</p>
