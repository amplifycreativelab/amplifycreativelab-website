<p>
  <strong>What is a 90‑day content strategy?</strong> It’s a systematic plan that turns a single event photography shoot into three months of consistent, engaging marketing material. For Perth venues, this means your opening night, product launch, or special event isn’t just a one‑off celebration—it’s a content engine that can drive bookings, boost social media engagement, and strengthen your local SEO for an entire quarter.
</p>

<p>
  According to a 2024 study by Hospitality Trends Australia, venues that repurposed professional event photos across multiple channels saw a <strong>40% higher engagement rate</strong> on social media and a <strong>25% increase in website traffic</strong> over the following 90 days. Yet most Perth restaurants, bars, and event spaces still treat photography as a short‑term cost rather than a long‑term investment. <h2>Why a 90‑Day Strategy Beats “One‑and‑Done” Photography</h2>

<p>
  When you hire a photographer for your event, you’re not just paying for images of that night. You’re buying a visual library that can be sliced, diced, and repurposed across every marketing touchpoint for months. The alternative—relying on sporadic smartphone snaps or stock imagery—leaves you with a disjointed, low‑impact presence that fails to tell a consistent brand story.
</p>

<p>
  Here’s what a deliberate 90‑day content strategy delivers that ad‑hoc photography doesn’t:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Consistent brand narrative:</strong> Every post, email, and webpage reinforces the same visual identity, building recognition and trust.</li>
  <li><strong>Higher ROI on your photography investment:</strong> Instead of using images once, you reuse them across dozens of campaigns, effectively lowering your cost per use.</li>
  <li><strong>Improved local SEO:</strong> Fresh, locally relevant images on your Google Business Profile and website signal to Google that your venue is active and engaging, boosting your search rankings.</li>
  <li><strong>Reduced content‑creation pressure:</strong> With a bank of professional photos ready to go, you’re never scrambling for something to post on social media or include in your newsletter.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Timeline: How to Stretch One Night Into 90 Days of Content</h2>

<p>
  A successful 90‑day strategy follows a deliberate calendar. Below is a week‑by‑week breakdown of how to deploy your event photos for maximum impact.
</p>

<h3>Week 1‑2: The Launch Window</h3>

<p>
  Capitalise on the immediate buzz of your event. Use images to:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Post‑event recaps:</strong> Share highlights on Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn with a “thank you” to attendees.</li>
  <li><strong>Press and media kits:</strong> Supply local publications (The West Australian, Scoop, Perth food blogs) with professional shots to secure coverage.</li>
  <li><strong>Google Business Profile updates:</strong> Upload 5‑10 of the best atmosphere and detail shots to your profile—this can increase profile views by up to 35% according to Google data.</li>
  <li><strong>Website hero rotation:</strong> Swap your homepage banner to a vibrant event photo that shows your venue at its most lively.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Week 3‑8: The Deep‑Dive Phase</h3>

<p>
  Now that the initial excitement has settled, start telling deeper stories with your imagery:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Feature individual dishes and drinks:</strong> Use close‑up shots from the event to highlight menu items in weekly social‑media posts.</li>
  <li><strong>Behind‑the‑scenes storytelling:</strong> Share candid shots of your team in action, along with captions that humanise your brand.</li>
  <li><strong>Email marketing sequences:</strong> Incorporate event photos into welcome emails, promotional offers, and loyalty‑program announcements.</li>
  <li><strong>“Throwback” content:</strong> Re‑share standout images with new captions or customer testimonials to keep the event alive in followers’ feeds.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Month 3: The Long‑Tail Leverage</h3>

<p>
  By the third month, your event is no longer news, but the photos are still fresh to anyone who hasn’t seen them. This is the time to:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Create evergreen website pages:</strong> Build a dedicated “Events” or “Gallery” page that showcases your venue’s capability to host functions.</li>
  <li><strong>Seasonal promotions:</strong> Tie event images to upcoming holidays or seasons (e.g., “Remember our winter launch? Our new summer menu is here!”).</li>
  <li><strong>Paid social ads:</strong> Use the highest‑performing organic posts as the creative for targeted Facebook/Instagram ads aimed at new audiences.</li>
  <li><strong>Print and collateral:</strong> Produce physical menus, flyers, or loyalty cards that feature real‑life atmosphere shots from the event.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Plan Your Event Photography for Maximum Content</h2>

<p>
  To extract 90 days of content from a single shoot, you need to think strategically before the photographer arrives. Follow these steps:
</p>

<ol>
  <li><strong>Create a detailed shot list:</strong> Identify must‑have moments (ribbon‑cutting, first toast, signature‑dish presentation) and key details (decor, lighting, branded elements).</li>
  <li><strong>Capture variety:</strong> Ensure your photographer gets wide‑angle atmosphere shots, medium‑group candids, tight detail shots, and empty‑venue “beauty” shots before guests arrive.</li>
  <li><strong>Think beyond the party:</strong> Ask for shots that can be used in future promotions—seasonal decorations, empty tables set for different group sizes, close‑ups of ingredients or glassware.</li>
  <li><strong>Coordinate with your team:</strong> Make sure staff know the photographer is there and encourage them to facilitate natural moments rather than stiff poses.</li>
  <li><strong>Discuss usage rights and turnaround:</strong> Confirm that you have the rights to use the images across all marketing channels, and agree on a delivery timeline that supports your 90‑day calendar.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Common Mistakes Perth Venues Make (And How to Avoid Them)</h2>

<p>
  Even venues that invest in professional photography often miss the opportunity to maximise its value. Steer clear of these pitfalls:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Using images once and forgetting them:</strong> Don’t let your photos gather digital dust. Schedule them into your content calendar so they’re working for you long after the event.</li>
  <li><strong>Neglecting SEO optimisation:</strong> Upload images with descriptive, keyword‑rich filenames and alt text (e.g., “candid‑guests‑laughing‑northbridge‑bar‑opening‑night.jpg” rather than “DSC1234.jpg”). This helps your venue appear in Google Image Search and improves overall site SEO.</li>
  <li><strong>Over‑editing or using heavy filters:</strong> Authenticity builds trust. Keep edits natural so the images reflect the real experience customers can expect.</li>
  <li><strong>Ignoring platform‑specific specs:</strong> Each channel (Instagram, Google, your website) has ideal image dimensions and file sizes. Deliver images that are optimised for each use case to ensure they display perfectly.</li>
  <li><strong>Failing to track performance:</strong> Use UTM parameters, booking codes, or promo links to measure which images drive the most engagement and conversions. This data will inform your future content strategy.</li>
</ul>
