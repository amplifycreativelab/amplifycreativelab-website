<h2>Step 1 – The "Packshot" (Product Photography)</h2>
<p>
  Whether you're launching packaged goods—sauces, beans, brews, ready-to-drink cocktails—or a new venue, your visual assets need to be ready before anything else. Professional product photography isn't just about making things look pretty; it's about creating consistency across every touchpoint where customers will encounter your brand.
</p>

<p>
  For packaged goods and CPG products, you'll need:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Clean e-commerce shots on white:</strong> These are your hero images for online retailers, your website, and marketplace listings. They need to be crisp, properly lit, and meet each platform's technical requirements.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Lifestyle shots in context:</strong> Show your products being used in authentic Perth settings—on tables at local cafés, in outdoor entertaining spaces, at picnics in Kings Park, at beach barbecues in Cottesloe.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Scale and detail shots:</strong> Capture close-ups of textures, ingredients, and unique features. For food products, show the consistency, the garnish, the way steam rises from a freshly prepared serving.
  </li>
</ul>

<h2>Step 4 – Local SEO & Google Maps</h2>
<p>
  Once your physical presence is established, your digital presence needs to match. Local SEO isn't just about ranking—it's about being found when hungry customers are making immediate decisions about where to eat or drink.
</p>

<p>
  Essential local SEO steps for Perth businesses:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>Verify your Google Business Profile:</strong> This is your most important local marketing asset. Complete every section—services, attributes, photos, hours, and detailed business information.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Add consistent NAP details:</strong> Your Name, Address, and Phone number must be identical across your website, Google Business Profile, social media, and any directory listings. Inconsistencies confuse both customers and search engines.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Upload professional photos regularly:</strong> Use those product shots and event photos to keep your Google Business Profile fresh. Google favors businesses that regularly update their profiles with high-quality, relevant images.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Encourage and respond to reviews:</strong> Make it easy for customers to leave reviews by creating QR codes that link directly to your review page. Respond to every review—both positive and negative—to show that you're engaged and care about customer feedback.
  </li>
</ul>

<ul>

  <li>
    <strong>Customer-generated content strategy:</strong> Encourage customers to share their own photos and experiences. Create Instagram-worthy spots in your venue, develop shareable moments, and actively engage with user-generated content.

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions: WA Food & Venue Launches</h2>

<aside className="faq-section">
  <h3>How far in advance should we start preparing for launch?</h3>
  <p>
    Begin <strong>8–12 weeks before your target launch date</strong>. This allows time for photography (weeks 8–10), website development (weeks 6–8), SEO groundwork (weeks 4–6), and pre-launch buzz building (weeks 1–4). Rushed timelines are possible but reduce impact.
  </p>

  <h3>What's the minimum budget for a coordinated launch?</h3>
  <p>
    For a lean but effective launch including photography, website, and local SEO setup, budget <strong>$5,000–$8,000</strong>. For a comprehensive launch with event photography and influencer coordination, budget <strong>$10,000–$15,000</strong>. This investment typically pays for itself within 2–3 months.
  </p>

  <h3>Should we focus on social media or the website first?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Always website first.</strong> Your social media will drive traffic, but without a professional website to land on, you'll lose conversions. The website is your owned media; social platforms can change algorithms anytime. Build your digital home first, then amplify through social channels.
  </p>

  <h3>How important is influencer marketing for Perth launches?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Very important.</strong> Perth's hospitality scene is tight-knit; local food influencers have highly engaged, relevant audiences. Micro-influencers (2,000–15,000 followers) often deliver better ROI than larger accounts. Focus on authentic relationships rather than transactional posts.
  </p>

  <h3>What if we're launching with a small menu or limited offering?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Quality over quantity.</strong> Better to have 10 stunning photos of your core offering than 50 mediocre shots. Focus photography on your signature items and hero dishes. You can expand your visual library as the menu grows, but launch with polished, professional coverage of what you do best.
  </p>

  <h3>How do we get press coverage for our Perth launch?</h3>
  <p>
    Three keys: (1) <strong>Professional photos</strong>—journalists won't cover you without them; (2) <strong>A compelling story angle</strong>—what's unique about you in the Perth market; (3) <strong>Easy access to assets</strong>—create a simple press kit with high-res images, founder bios, and key facts. Local publications like Scoop, So Perth, and food bloggers are your primary targets.
  </p>

  <h3>Should we soft launch before the official launch?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Yes—with a caveat.</strong> A soft launch lets you work out operational issues before inviting press and influencers. But keep it truly soft—friends, family, industry contacts. Don't invite photographers or media until you're confident in the experience. Your official launch should be the polished, photo-ready moment.
  </p>
