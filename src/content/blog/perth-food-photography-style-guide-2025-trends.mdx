<h3>Coastal Vibes (Fremantle, Scarborough, Cottesloe)</h3>
<p>
  Bright, airy compositions with plenty of natural light, beach‑inspired props, and an emphasis on fresh seafood and casual dining appeal. Think soft shadows, blue‑toned backgrounds, and a sense of openness that mirrors the coastal environment. This style resonates strongly with tourists and locals alike who associate the WA coast with quality seafood and relaxed dining.
</p>

<h3>Industrial Chic (Perth CBD, Northbridge)</h3>
<p>
  Dramatic lighting, dark backgrounds, and sophisticated compositions that make dishes pop. Perfect for cocktail bars, laneway venues, and fine‑dining establishments, this style uses contrast and texture to create a premium, urban feel. It plays well on Instagram where bold visuals cut through crowded feeds.
</p>

<h3>Rustic & Earthy (Mount Lawley, Leederville)</h3>
<p>
  Warm, natural tones, authentic styling, and a focus on artisanal, locally‑sourced ingredients. This approach suits wine bars, intimate venues, brunch spots, and any restaurant that wants to highlight its connection to WA's producers and the farm-to-table movement.
</p>

<h2>2. Bold Lighting Techniques</h2>
<p>
  While natural light remains a favourite for its freshness and authenticity, 2025 sees a rise in controlled artificial lighting—especially flash photography—used to create dramatic, attention‑grabbing images.
</p>

<p>
  The "flash‑on‑camera" look, once avoided by professionals, is now embraced for its raw, energetic feel that performs exceptionally well on social media. Posts with high‑contrast lighting receive <strong>40% more engagement</strong> on Instagram than evenly‑lit alternatives.
</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions: Perth Food Photography Trends</h2>

<aside className="faq-section">
  <h3>What's the biggest food photography trend for 2025?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Location-driven authenticity.</strong> Perth diners want to see photos that genuinely reflect the venue's suburb and vibe—not generic stock imagery. Whether you're coastal in Fremantle or industrial in Northbridge, your photography should feel true to your setting.
  </p>

  <h3>Is natural light or flash better for food photography?</h3>
  <p>
    Both have their place in 2025. <strong>Natural light</strong> remains ideal for brunch, café, and daytime venues—it creates fresh, authentic visuals. <strong>Flash and dramatic lighting</strong> are trending for bars, nightlife, and fine dining, delivering high-contrast images that perform well on social media (40% higher engagement).
  </p>

  <h3>Do I really need video content for my restaurant?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Yes—in 2025, video is essential.</strong> Short-form video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) is how younger audiences discover venues. Simple videos—pour shots, garnishing, kitchen behind-scenes—don't require big budgets but significantly extend your reach.
  </p>

  <h3>How important is sustainability in food photography?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Increasingly important.</strong> Perth diners, especially younger demographics, actively seek out venues with sustainable practices. Photography that highlights local sourcing, zero-waste plating, and ethical production builds trust and differentiates your brand.
  </p>

  <h3>What photography style suits my Perth suburb?</h3>
  <p>
    <strong>Match your location:</strong> Coastal venues (Fremantle, Cottesloe, Scarborough) suit bright, airy, seafood-focused imagery. Urban venues (Northbridge, CBD) suit dark, dramatic, high-contrast styling. Brunch spots (Mount Lawley, Leederville) suit warm, rustic, artisanal visuals.
  </p>

  <h3>How often should I update my food photography?</h3>
  <p>
    Update when you change menus seasonally (4 times per year for most Perth venues). For social media, aim for fresh content <strong>3–5 times per week</strong>. Your core website photography should be refreshed annually or whenever dishes significantly change.
  </p>

  <h3>What equipment do I need for simple video content?</h3>
  <p>
    For basic short-form video: a <strong>tripod, smartphone with good camera, and natural lighting</strong>. For higher quality: a stabiliser/gimbal and possibly an external microphone. Most 2025 trends favour authentic, slightly raw content—professional cinema quality isn't required.
  </p>
</aside>
